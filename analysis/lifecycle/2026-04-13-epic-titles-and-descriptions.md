# Lifecycle Email Epics — Titles & Descriptions

## Context

The 2026-03-31 brainstorm stickies were regrouped into epic boxes on `initiatives/lifecycle-email-brainstorm/updated_brainstorm.png` and split per stage into seven files in `analysis/lifecycle/brainstorm-grouped/` (acquire.png ... crosscutting.png). ## ACQUIRE — stranger → sign-up

Goal: Convert anonymous interest into known contacts, then into trials or subscriptions.

### Epic 1 — Interactive Product Walkthroughs
In-product and embedded walkthroughs of Search & Discovery features (Supe, Reference Track), delivered as guided UI tours and as Loom/Zoom intro videos with a real named person on camera (not a faceless AI avatar) so anonymous visitors see what the product does before committing to signup.

**Stickies:**

- Search walk-thru to find the perfect track (Dave Karl)
- Interactive product walk-through for Search & Discovery features (Steven Davis)
- Loom or Zoom product intro videos with a face and a name, e.g. George (Steven Davis)

### Epic 2 — SoundBot Intent Capture
In-app conversational assistant that either asks what a visitor came for or infers it from behavior, then surfaces the right content. Most common question ("which license is the right one?") gets pre-baked answers, with examples.

**Stickies:**

- Have questions? Ask SoundBot with examples on common questions, especially licensing (Steven Davis)
- Figure out what they came for -- ask or infer -- and surface information to fit their specific need (Steven Davis)

### Epic 3 — Persona-Based Plans & Landing Pages
Dedicated plans and landing pages for distinct buyer personas (YouTuber, podcaster, filmmaker, agency, enterprise), each tuned to the pricing, proof points, and content that matter to that role, rather than a single generic funnel.

**Stickies:**

- Persona-based plans and/or landing pages (Steven Davis)

### Epic 4 — Referral Program
Two-sided incentive program that rewards existing customers (e.g., one free month for every friend who subscribes) and gives the referred friend a signup incentive. Mechanics designed around mutual benefit.

**Stickies:**

- Referral program (Dave Karl)
- Build a referral program with benefits for current customer and who they refer (Steven Davis)

### Ungrouped stickies (bottom row, not yet boxed)
These three stickies sit loose below the 2x2 ACQUIRE grid. Each is a distinct top-of-funnel idea; they may warrant a 5th epic ("Segment-Specific Acquisition Hooks") or be split across Epics 1-4. Decision point before Step 5.

- College student plan (e.g. USD 49.99/year), validated via .edu email, with a post-graduation path to Pro/Creator (Steven Davis).
- Using the Discover block on-site to promote real customer placements or projects (Dave Karl).
- Use Supe or Reference Track to offer an "opposite track" option -- something really out of the box (Steven Davis).

## ACTIVATE — registered → paid

Goal: Get new subscribers to experience core value (first download, first project use) as fast as possible.

### Epic 1 — Role & Licensing Onboarding Branching
Segment new signups by use case at registration and route each into a short licensing questionnaire that lands them on the correct commercial option (single-song license vs. subscription tier). All subsequent onboarding emails tailored to the selected role.

**Stickies:**

- "Getting Started by Role" Branching (Devon)
- Interactive licensing questionnaire similar to old onboarding (Steven Davis)

### Epic 2 — Day 0-30 Behavioral Onboarding Sequence
Unified onboarding flow spanning enterprise, subscription, and transactional users, personalized by industry/use case. Sends triggered by behavioral gaps (hasn't browsed, searched, downloaded, used in project) rather than calendar days. Includes A/B testing of sender identity (specific person like George vs. marketing-brand vs. embedded-in-marketing, following the pattern Taylor built for enterprise).

**Stickies:**

- Consistent onboarding for new users across enterprise/subs/transactional, personalized by industry (Sourav Nayak)
- Day 0-30 Onboarding Sequence (Devon)
- Test different onboarding personas -- George vs Marketing vs embedded (Steven Davis)

### Epic 3 — Feature Discovery Series
Staggered, trigger-based introductions to features a user hasn't tried yet -- playlists, favoriting, stems, SFX library, Premiere Pro plugin. One feature per email, sent only when the feature remains unused. Targeted at repeat visitors who've registered but haven't explored.

**Stickies:**

- Understand if there's a key feature they haven't used, especially for repeat visitors (Steven Davis)
- Feature Discovery Series (Devon)

### Epic 4 — Checkout & Pricing Abandonment Triggers
Recovery flows for users who abandon at the pricing page, subscription checkout, or cart. Each abandonment point gets its own sequence addressing the likely blocker at that step (price confusion, licensing uncertainty, missing value signal).

**Stickies:**

- Cart abandonment (Dave Karl)
- Subscription checkout page abandonment (Dave Karl)
- Pricing page visit abandonment with promotional offer (Dave Karl)

### Epic 5 — Repeat-Visitor Conversion Intelligence
Diagnose and convert self-service visitors who return multiple times without subscribing. Segment by visit frequency and behavior; identify who converts vs. who doesn't and why; deploy targeted discount offers to high-intent non-converters.

**Stickies:**

- Offers/discounts to self-service users who return but haven't converted (Sourav Nayak)
- Understand repeat visitors, their frequency and behaviors (Steven Davis)

### Ungrouped stickies (bottom row, not yet boxed)
Three stickies below the grid. Candidate 6th epic ("Nurture, Retargeting & Quick-Win Content") -- mid-funnel re-engagement across paid and organic channels -- or fold into existing epics.

- Content downloads, event attendees, webinar registrant re-engagement/nurture (Dave Karl).
- Re-targeting ads: pink banners with song name / artist names users interacted with (Steven Davis).
- Quick Win Templates -- "Your first video soundtrack in 3 minutes" (Devon).

## ENGAGE — healthy usage

Goal: Deepen habitual usage patterns that make the product sticky.

### Epic 1 — Usage Milestones & Gamification
Behavioral and milestone-triggered recognition: first download, 10th/50th/100th download, monthly usage summaries, cross-library discovery triggers (downloader gets SFX intro, SFX user gets music intro), and gamified incentives (discounts for maintaining a monthly usage threshold). Reinforces habit by celebrating activity and suggesting the next step.

**Stickies:**

- Monthly usage summary emails
- Cross-Library Discovery (Devon)
- Gamification: monthly usage thresholds with discounts (Sourav Nayak)
- Usage Milestone Emails -- 10/50/100 (Devon)
- First Download Celebration (Devon)
- Gamifications when applicable -- first, 25th, 100th song (Sourav Nayak)
- Segmented one-off emails based on engagement level (Dave Karl)

### Epic 2 — Editorial, Social Proof & Product Updates
Editorial cadence that keeps active users aware of expanding catalog and platform value: seasonal/trend content pushes (holiday music, awards-season scores, social-media trending genres); monthly "What's New" product update; peer/competitor listening patterns; deep-cut catalog promotion (quirky, out-of-the-box music); customer success highlights of where Soundstripe music has been used.

**Stickies:**

- Seasonal/Trend Content Pushes (Devon)
- "What's New" Product Updates (Devon)
- Recommendations on what other people/companies listen to
- Deep cuts promotion (Steven Davis)
- Email all customers highlights of where our music has been successful (Sourav Nayak)

### Epic 3 — Personalized Recommendation Digests
Personalized recommendation engine delivered by email: a weekly/bi-weekly new-release digest tuned to each user's download history, genre preferences, and search patterns; genre-threshold outreach when a user crosses N downloads in a style; content refreshed when new releases match their favorite genre/artist or when new features apply to them.

**Stickies:**

- Personalized song recommendations based on search (Dave Karl)
- More than X songs of a specific genre → outreach with additional playlists (Dave Karl)
- New Release Digest (Personalized) (Devon)
- Personalization to existing subs highlighting new releases in their favorite genre/artist + new features (Sourav Nayak)

### Epic 4 — Advocacy & Community Amplification
Turn healthy users into advocates: enroll high-NPS and power users into an ambassador/affiliate program, trigger emails when users share a playlist or invite a collaborator, spotlight creator case studies ("See how [creator] used Soundstripe"), and encourage project sharing as a lightweight advocacy signal.

**Stickies:**

- Advocacy Program Enrollment for high-NPS/power users (Devon)
- Campaign to encourage project sharing
- Community/Social Proof Spotlights (Devon)
- Shared Playlist / Collaboration Trigger (Devon)

### Epic 5 — Active-User Health Reinforcement
Micro-flows keyed to healthy-usage signals: reinforce active downloaders to prevent lapse, nudge active browsers who aren't downloading with curated recs, and remind users who favorited tracks but didn't download ("your favorites are waiting"). Deepens the habit before any lapse signal appears.

**Stickies:**

- Active Downloader → Value Reinforcement (Devon)
- Favorited but Didn't Download (Devon)
- Active Browser → Download Nudge (Devon)

## RETAIN — at-risk signals

Goal: Intervene before cancellation.

### Epic 1 — Lapse & Cancel-Intent Intercepts
Graduated intervention tied to inactivity thresholds (30, 60, 180 days) and explicit cancel-intent signals (cancel-page visit, cancel CTA click). Early-lapse emails emphasize feature discovery or downgrade paths; deep-lapse emails lead with major updates and value reminders. App-engagement telemetry triggers the appropriate stage automatically.

**Stickies:**

- 30 days on inactivity triggers reminder of new music
- Use app engagement to trigger reengagement email campaigns
- Cancel Intent Intercept (Devon)
- Early Lapse Re-engagement 31-60 days (Devon)
- Deep Lapse Win-back 61-180 days (Devon)

### Epic 2 — Dead-End Session Recovery
Email follow-ups when a session stalls: user engaged Supe or a reference track without downloading, or searched and got zero/poor results. Recovery message offers similar tracks, intentionally opposite tracks, alternative keywords, or trending material in related genres to restart the intent.

**Stickies:**

- Kinda/sorts cart abandonment for sessions that touched Supe/reference track without downloading (Steven Davis)
- Search With No Results Follow-Up (Devon)

### Epic 3 — Renewal Value Recap
Renewal-period cadence that recaps each subscriber's year of realized value -- total downloads, top genres, catalog consumption -- anchoring the renewal decision against what they got, not list price.

**Stickies:**

- Renewal cadence reminding users of all the value they've seen in the last year (Dave Karl)

### Epic 4 — Enterprise Usage Drop Alerts (AE Enablement)
Internal AE-facing automated emails that surface enterprise account health -- zero-download months, unused seats, usage drops quarter-over-quarter -- so AEs can intervene with targeted outreach or feature education before renewal risk compounds. Not customer-facing.

**Stickies:**

- Enterprise: drop-off detection with targeted outreach (Sourav Nayak)
- AE Enablement Emails (Devon)

## WINBACK — churned recovery

Goal: Re-engage former subscribers with the right offer at the right time.

### Epic 1 — Win-Back Offer Ladder (multi-trigger)
Core winback epic that stacks four trigger types: an immediate post-cancel series (Day 0 "sorry to see you go" / Day 7 "what's new since you left" / Day 30 offer); a general offer ladder that escalates incentives (come-back → free month → discount on annual) by elapsed time since churn; a seasonal cadence for former subscribers in seasonal industries (re-engage 4-6 weeks before their peak season); and trigger-based re-engagement when a former subscriber returns to the site or a major product launch happens.

**Stickies:**

- Win back to previous subscribers (Dave Karl)
- Win-Back Offer Ladder (Devon)
- Prior Subscribers -- aggressive annual offers via email
- Seasonal Winback (Devon)
- Trigger-Based Winback (Devon)
- Immediate Post-Cancel Series (Devon)

### Epic 2 — Plan Downgrade Save
When a user downgrades plan tier, acknowledge the change, flag what capability they lose, offer a clear "come back to [plan] anytime" path, and monitor for re-upgrade triggers. Treats downgrade as a state to recover from, not a final outcome.

**Stickies:**

- Plan Downgrade Save (Devon)

### Ungrouped stickies (top of frame, not yet boxed)
Two stickies sit above the main WINBACK box. They target non-subscription relationships and could form a 3rd epic ("Lapsed Lead & Transactional Re-Acquisition").

- Prior transactional customers -- personalized messages for repurchase with aggressive catalog offers (Sourav Nayak).
- Segmentation and re-engagement to old MQLs/PQLs -- business plan fits? New feature promotion? (Dave Karl).

## CROSS-CUTTING

Goal: Infrastructure and strategy items that make the whole program work together.

### Epic 1 — Behavioral Segmentation & Seasonal Detection
Unified segmentation layer: granular segmentation by purchase type, recency, and activity level; a seasonal-vs-steady user detector that adjusts cadence around each user's peak season (wedding videographers, holiday creators) instead of flagging predictable off-season quiet as lapse; and a trigger library spanning time on platform, preview counts, download counts, sequential-month visits, and random-month return patterns across songs/SFX/video.

**Stickies:**

- More granular segmentation
- Segmentation of users by purchase type and recency/activity (Sourav Nayak)
- Seasonal vs. Steady User Detection (Devon)
- Triggers to leverage -- time on platform, previews, downloads, visit sequences (Steven Davis)

### Epic 2 — Email Hygiene & Verification
Data-quality infrastructure: verify incoming addresses at capture to remove junk domains (qwerty@none.com) that waste send budget and degrade deliverability; detect student (k-12) email patterns (##@domain.edu, domain.k12.edu) to route or suppress appropriately. Feeds health signals back to acquisition to improve lead quality at the top of funnel.

**Stickies:**

- Verify emails -- junk addresses waste money/space and email verification improves deliverability (Steven Davis)
- Build in systems to remove student (k-12) emails via domain-format detection (Steven Davis)

### Epic 3 — Stage Tagging, Suppression & New-vs-Tenured Cadence
Foundational orchestration: every contact carries a lifecycle_stage property synced between product DB, CRM, and email platform (foundation for every other flow); a global suppression and orchestration layer (max emails/week per contact, cross-flow priority hierarchy, AE-sequence suppression for enterprise accounts, cooldowns between flows -- the "traffic cop"); and differential cadence for new (0-90 day) subscribers (onboarding-heavy) vs. tenured subscribers (lower frequency, value reinforcement) to avoid over-educating long-time users.

**Stickies:**

- Suppression & Orchestration Layer (Devon)
- Lifecycle Stage Tagging Infrastructure (Devon)
- New vs. Tenured Subscriber Cadence (Devon)

### Epic 4 — In-App ↔ Email Mirroring & Preference Control
Cross-channel coordination layer: in-app messaging mirrors one-off email content so channels reinforce rather than fragment; a revamped preference center gives users explicit control over contact topics and frequency; in-app reminders alert users when emails are being dispatched to their inbox.

**Stickies:**

- Better use of in-app messaging mirroring one-off emails (Dave Karl)
- Revisit preference center to give users more control over contact (Dave Karl)
- In-app reminder of emails coming to inbox (Dave Karl)

### Epic 5 — Contact Lifecycle Management & Sunset
Rules for entering and exiting the active marketing contact list: systematic opt-out removal; enrollment/removal based on site-visit recency (e.g., 90-day active window); and a dormant sunset at 180+ days that attempts a final re-engagement email before moving the contact to suppressed state.

**Stickies:**

- Marketing contacts -- systematically remove opt-outs (Steven Davis)
- Enroll & remove from active marketing contacts based on site-visit frequency, 90 days (Steven Davis)
- Dormant Sunset 180+ days (Devon)
